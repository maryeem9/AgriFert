RABI CROPS

Rabi crops are a type of agricultural crops that are sown and harvested during the winter season, which typically spans from October to March in Pakistan. These crops are well-adapted to cooler temperatures and require relatively less water compared to other crop seasons.

Here are some examples of Rabi crops: Wheat: Wheat is one of the most important Rabi crops. It is a staple food crop and widely cultivated during the winter season. India is one of the largest producers of wheat in the world.
Barley: Barley is another important Rabi crop. It is primarily used for animal feed, but it is also used in the production of beer and other alcoholic beverages.
Gram (Chickpea): Mustard: Mustard is a Rabi oilseed crop that is grown for its edible oil. It is used for cooking purposes and is also used in the production of mustard sauce and condiments.
Gram (Chickpea): Gram, also known as chickpea or Bengal gram, is a pulse crop grown during the winter season. It is a rich source of protein and is used in various culinary preparations, such as curries, soups, and snacks.
Peas:Peas are a popular vegetable crop grown in the Rabi season. They are used in various dishes, salads, and soups.
Lentils: Lentils are another type of pulse crop grown during the Rabi season. They are rich in protein and commonly used in Indian cuisine.
